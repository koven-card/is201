Ultimate/AUDL Rules
AUDL Rules

Field Size

The AUDL field is played on football sized field with the endzones being twice as large as normal football endzones. The goal of the game is to move the disc(frisbee) up the field and catch it in the endzone. While you are holding the disc, you cannot move, but are allowed a pivot.

Stall

When you have the disc, you can only hold it for a certain amount of time. This is refered to as the stall count. In the AUDL, the stall count is seven seconds, or in other words, you can only hold the disc for seven seconds before you have to throw it to another player.

Fouls

Ultimate is a non-contact sport, so there is no tackling or pushing other players. There are referees on the field in the AUDL that count stall, watch for fouls, and call turnovers. The result of a foul is a 10 or 20 years advance or step back the field for the team with the disc. Referees and a reward for fouls are unique to the AUDL and are not part of other ultimate organizations.

Timeline of Major Events

Expansion of the AUDL

  • 2012 Season

    Beginnings of the AUDL

    The AUDL started in 2012 with its first game on April 14th of that year. At that time, the AUDL only had 8 teams, broken up into an Eastern Division and a Western Devision. The first two years didn't start well for the AUDL, during which the league ended up losing 4 of those 8 beginning teams, but the AUDL was here to stay.

  • 2013-2016 Seasons

    AUDL Growth

    These years were growth years for the AUDL amid this increasingly more popular sport. From 4 teams at the beginning of 2013, the league expanded to include 25 teams in 4 devisions, spread all across the US.

  • 2022 Season

    Three expansion teams introduced

    For the first time since 2016, (with the exception of one added team in 2020) more teams were being introduced into the AUDL team roster. The three teams were all added into the Western Devision, which, at that point, only had 4 teams. The three teams were: the Salt Lake Shred, the Colorado Summit, and the Portland Nitro.

  • 2023 Season

    One more expansion team introduced

    Starting in the 2023 season, one more expansion team will be introduced in Houston, Texas. This team does not yet have a name.

Western Division

Seven Teams

The Western Division has the seven following teams: Colorado Summit, Los Angeles Aviators, Oakland Spiders, Portland Nitro, Salt Lake Shred, San Diego Growlers, and Seattle Cascades

Central Division

Six Teams

The Central Division has the six following teams: Chicago Union, Detroit Mechanix, Indianapolis AlleyCats, Madison Radicals, Minnesota Wind Chill, and Pittsburgh Thunderbirds

Southern Division

Five Teams

The Southern Division has the five following teams: Atlanta Hustle, Austin Sol, Carolina Flyers, Dallas Legion, and a Houston team.

AUDL All-Stars Game

The All-Stars Game takes the best players from all the divsions and puts them on two teams in a friendly competition game. To watch the game or learn more, check out https://www.theaudl.com/league/allstar.
